Breast Cancer Classifications

Understanding the different classifications of breast cancer is crucial for accurate diagnosis and treatment.

Benign

Benign breast conditions are non-cancerous. They do not spread to other parts of the body and are usually not life-threatening.

Malignant

Malignant breast cancer is cancerous and can spread to other parts of the body. Early detection and treatment are crucial.

Normal

Normal breast tissue shows no signs of cancer. Regular screening is important for early detection of any abnormalities.

About Breast Cancer

Breast cancer is one of the most common types of cancer affecting women worldwide. It originates in the breast tissue and can spread to other parts of the body if not detected early. Symptoms may include lumps in the breast, changes in breast shape or size, and abnormal discharge from the nipple. Early detection through regular screenings and self-exams is crucial for effective treatment and improved outcomes. If you or someone you know is at risk, seeking medical advice and maintaining regular check-ups is essential for managing breast cancer effectively.
